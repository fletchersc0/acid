About ACID

A fundamental challenge in cognitive science is to explain how humans (and other agents) learn hidden structures, navigate uncertainty, and make decisions, often while subject to cognitive biases and limited computational resources. ACID is a collaboration of multiple universities meeting at RMIT that provides a dedicated platform for exploring these themes, with a focus on methodologies that illuminate learning, decision‑making processes, and their practical implications.

ACID brings together researchers from diverse disciplines including psychology, neuroscience, and computer science who share an interest in the mechanisms driving adaptive behaviour. We host informal presentations of ongoing work and encourage open discussion, offering space for constructive critique and collaborative exchange.

Who it's for

The forum is targeted towards HDR students. In particular, we've started a study group for PhD candidates researching computational cognitive science and decision‑making.

Weekly rhythm

We meet one day each week at RMIT (City Campus). Exact day/time and room details are announced via the mailing list.

A typical day

10:00–12:00Quiet co‑work
12:00–13:00Break / Lunch
13:00–14:00Skill‑share or problem‑share (short demo)
14:00–16:00Quiet co‑work

What to expect

  • Short, informal demos of ongoing work.
  • Open discussion and constructive critique.
  • Focus blocks for writing, analysis, and modelling.
  • Cross‑disciplinary methods and theory.

Researcher “White Pages”

Each demo contributes a small entry to a living White Pages: common problems/solutions documented by the group, plus early‑career researcher contacts. This index will help new members find people, tools, and prior solutions quickly.
